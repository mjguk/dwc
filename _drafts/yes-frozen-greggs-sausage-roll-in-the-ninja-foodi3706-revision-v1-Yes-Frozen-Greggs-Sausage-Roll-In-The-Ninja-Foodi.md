A treat for everyone. Here is how to cook a frozen Greggs sausage roll in the Ninja Foodi air fryer. This will save you time, money and get a Greggs sausage roll in your belly faster than you can say Steak Bake.

In my instructions I advise to flip the Greggs sausage roll a couple of times throughout the cooking process. This gives the opportunity to brown off on all sides equally and make for a nicer looking snack. Be careful when flipping it over, the inside of the Ninja Foodi is very hot so consider using some kitchen tongs to help you. I [recommend the bamboo or silicon type such as these](https://amzn.to/3kippYp) as metal ones can damage the inside of the air fryer.

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-medium">[![Frozen Greggs sausage roll in the ninja foodi air fryer](https://www.dadwhatcooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/1EFE7816-E023-47C3-A40C-8028AFB364BA-300x300.jpeg)](https://amzn.to/3LJ5q0R)</figure>### Try egg wash on your Greggs Sausage Roll

Using an egg wash will give your Greggs sausage roll a shiny brown finish once cooked. This can be achieved by cracking an egg into a bowl, add a pinch of salt and pepper and beat it with a fork. Once all broken down, use a pastry brush to paint on a thin layer of egg wash as the Greggs sausage rolls come out of the freezer before they go in the Ninja Foodi air fryer.

If you [use a silicon pastry brush like this one](https://amzn.to/3XqkG5e), then you can paint the egg wash directly on the Greggs sausage rolls when they are in the air fryer before you start to cook them.

### Can you Air Fry Vegan Greggs Sausage Rolls?

You can also use this same recipe for the vegan Greggs sausage roll in the Ninja Foodi, cooking times are exactly the same as the recipe below.

</div></div> </div></div></div>### Can you cook the Greggs Steak Bake in the Ninja Foodie Air Fryer?

The simple answer is yes. I found the best way to do this is to keep it in a bit of foil and place that on the crisp bake tray. Cooking again at 180C but for a bit longer – 22 minutes seems to be about right. You may want to turn over the steak bake half way through cooking to get even coverage (because of the foil).

Egg wash works well again, so try starting with it face down, then turn the right way up and egg wash for the final half of the cooking.
